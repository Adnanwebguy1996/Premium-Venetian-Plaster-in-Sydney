Venetian Plaster Guide

What is Venetian Plaster?

Venetian plaster is a wall and ceiling finish that originated in ancient Rome and was perfected in Venice. It consists of slaked lime, marble dust, and natural pigments, creating a finish that resembles polished marble or stone with remarkable depth and luminosity.

Benefits of Venetian Plaster

  • Creates a unique, luxurious appearance with depth and movement that changes with light
  • Highly durable and long-lasting, often outliving the buildings it adorns
  • Breathable, allowing moisture to evaporate naturally and preventing mold growth
  • Mold and mildew resistant due to its alkaline composition
  • Eco-friendly and non-toxic, made from natural materials
  • Can be repaired seamlessly if damaged, maintaining its beauty for generations

Our Application Process

Our skilled artisans apply Venetian plaster in multiple thin layers, burnishing each layer to create the signature polished finish. The process requires precision and expertise to achieve the desired texture and sheen, with each layer contributing to the depth and character of the final surface.

Maintenance Tips

Venetian plaster is relatively low maintenance. Simply dust with a soft cloth or use a mild, pH-neutral cleaner for deeper cleaning. Avoid abrasive cleaners or scrubbing pads that could damage the finish. With proper care, your Venetian plaster will maintain its beauty for decades.
